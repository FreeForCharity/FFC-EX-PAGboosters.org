Typical USAG Xcel Season Meet Schedule

Below is an overview of the general meets throughout a typical USAG Xcel gymnastics season. Specific dates and locations vary by year.

Judges Critique / Prep Meet

First Meet of the Season

This is the season opener where gymnasts perform their routines in front of judges who provide constructive feedback. It's a low-pressure environment designed to help athletes refine their skills before official competition begins.

  • Purpose: Receive feedback from judges to improve routines
  • Timing: Early in the season (typically late fall/early winter)
  • Format: Informal scoring with detailed critiques

Regular Season Meets

4 Competitive Meets

Throughout the season, gymnasts compete in approximately four regular meets. These competitions allow athletes to showcase their skills, earn scores, and gain valuable competitive experience.

  • Location: Typically within a 2-3 hour drive of State College, PA
  • Format: Official USAG Xcel competition with scoring
  • Purpose: Build skills, gain experience, and qualify for championship meets
  • Timing: Spread throughout the winter season (December - February)

Fly Away Meet

Optional Destination Competition

The Fly Away Meet is an optional competition held at a more distant location, offering gymnasts the opportunity to compete in a different environment and make it a fun team trip.

  • Optional: Participation is not required
  • Location Examples: Phoenix, Arizona • Tampa, Florida • Other destination cities
  • Benefits: Team bonding, new competition experience, and travel opportunity
  • Timing: Typically mid-season

State Championship

Pennsylvania State Competition

The State Championship brings together Pennsylvania gymnasts from all Xcel levels. All levels compete at States, making it an inclusive championship event.

  • Location: Always held in Pennsylvania
  • All Levels Compete: Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, and Sapphire all participate
  • Qualification Scores (current season - verify with PA USAG for updates):
    • Bronze: 32.00 All-Around
    • Silver: 35.00 All-Around
    • Gold: 35.00 All-Around
    • Platinum: 35.00 All-Around
    • Diamond: 34.00 All-Around
    • Sapphire: 32.00 All-Around
    • Note: All Senior gymnasts automatically qualify regardless of score
  • Important: Qualifying scores may change each season - always verify current requirements with PA Women's Gymnastics
  • Timing: Late winter/early spring (typically March/April)

Regional Championship

Multi-State Competition

Gymnasts in Gold, Platinum, Diamond, and Sapphire levels who qualify at the State Championship advance to compete against athletes from other states in their region.

  • Region: Pennsylvania is in Region 7
    • Region 7 includes: Pennsylvania, Delaware, Maryland, New Jersey, Virginia, West Virginia, and District of Columbia
  • Location: Rotates among Region 7 states
  • Eligible Levels:
    • Gold: Must achieve qualifying All-Around score at State Championship
    • Platinum: Must achieve qualifying All-Around score at State Championship
    • Diamond: Must achieve qualifying All-Around score at State Championship
    • Sapphire: Must achieve qualifying All-Around score at State Championship
  • Bronze and Silver: Do NOT advance to Regionals - their season culminates at the State Championship
  • Qualification: Specific qualifying scores from States are set by each region and may vary by year
  • Timing: Spring (typically April/May)

About National Championships

Understanding Xcel Competition Structure

Important Note: There is currently no USAG Xcel National Championship. For Xcel gymnasts, the Regional Championship is the culminating competition of the season.

  • Xcel Program: The competitive season ends at Regionals for all Xcel levels (Gold, Platinum, Diamond, and Sapphire)
  • National Championships: Only available for USAG Development Program (DP) Levels 9 and 10, which is a different competitive track than Xcel
  • Highest Level for Xcel: Regional Championship represents the highest level of competition for Xcel gymnasts
  • Season Culmination:
    • Bronze & Silver: State Championship
    • Gold, Platinum, Diamond & Sapphire: Regional Championship

The Xcel program is designed to provide gymnasts with a competitive experience while offering more flexibility than the Development Program. Regional Championships showcase the highest level of Xcel competition in each region.
